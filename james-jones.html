What is a JJI-Joist?

JJI-Joists are advanced engineered timber beams designed to offer superior performance and versatility compared to traditional solid timber. Manufactured from high-grade, finger-jointed softwood flanges combined with a 9mm thick Oriented Strand Board (OSB) web, these joists deliver a remarkable strength-to-weight ratio while remaining straight, uniform, and stable. Their engineered composition resists shrinking, warping, and twisting, making them highly reliable for modern construction projects. Available in depths of 220mm and 245mm, lengths of 10 and 12 metres, and flange widths of 47mm, 63mm, 72mm, and 97mm from C&W Berry, JJI-Joists are precision-engineered to UK preferred dimensions, enabling designers and builders to optimise structural performance while maintaining consistent quality. Accredited to PAS 2050, they also provide a sustainable building solution.

Beyond their structural reliability, JJI-Joists offer practical benefits that improve efficiency on site. Their lightweight construction and uniform dimensions reduce transport costs and simplify handling during installation, making them ideal for floor joists, rafters, purlins, and wall studs. James Jones’ Forrest plant produces the UK’s largest standard range of JJI-Joists, with over one million linear metres in stock, ensuring reliable lead times. Precision manufacturing minimises waste and supports cost-effective construction, while the engineered design allows longer spans and higher load-bearing capacity compared to traditional solid timber. This combination of strength, stability, and sustainability makes JJI-Joists a preferred choice for modern construction projects.

Understanding JJI-Joists

JJI-Joists are engineered timber I-beams, designed to deliver strength, stability, and efficiency in modern construction. Their performance is determined by a combination of dimensions, grades, and availability codes, which together dictate how the joist will behave in a building. The section below explores these factors in more detail.

1. Depth, Length, & Flange Width

Depth - The vertical height of the joist. Greater depth generally allows for longer spans and higher load-bearing capacity

Length - The standard lengths (10 & 12 metres) suit different floor or roof layouts. Longer joists reduce the number of supports needed

Flange Width - Engineered to provide the necessary bending and shear capacity. Wider flanges correspond to higher grades (D > C > B+ > A+)

2. Performance Grades & Internal Strength

Grade Flange Width Description
A+ 47 mm Lowest bending and shear capacity; suitable for shorter spans and lighter loads
B+ 63 mm Moderate capacity; suitable for typical domestic projects and moderate spans
C 72 mm Medium-high capacity; suitable for standard residential and light commercial projects
D 97 mm Highest bending, shear, and stiffness capacities; suitable for long spans and heavy loads

Higher-grade joists (D > C > B+ > A+) are engineered to carry greater loads and span longer distances without additional support.
